Course Description

This course is a successor to CS5001: Internet of Things with Applied Data Science offered in Spring 2020. In this Fall 2020 course which is geared toward the 6000 level, the following main differences, among others, will be introduced:

·       A flipped-classroom instructional strategy will be incorporated into some classes; see Canvas for details.

·       In the latter part of the course, lecture slides will be replaced by reading materials at more advanced levels; see Canvas for details.

 

Prerequisites:

A grade of "C" or higher in Comp Sci 2500, in one of Comp Sci 3610, Comp Sci 4601 or Comp Sci 5600, and in one of Stat 3113, Stat 3115, Stat 3117 or Stat 5643. Senior or Graduate standing.

Course Goals

  1. Develop a reliable understanding of Internet of Things and Data Science in general.
  2. Gain working knowledge and hands-on experience in IoT, such as the insights into the IoT protocol stack, IoT security, and prototype development using Raspberry Pi.
  3. Gain working knowledge and hands-on experience in data science, such as Python programming, data preparation using pandas, data visualization using matplotlib, and machine learning using scikit-learn.
  4. Acquire and enhance capability of conducting cutting-edge research in IoT, machine learning, and security; improve analytical and critical thinking ability to identify problems and come up with novel solutions.
